Instructor Assignment & D2L Roster Tool

Automating LMS setup and improving cross-campus communication

Overview

This Python-based utility streamlines post-scheduling administrative tasks by generating reports that map faculty assignments across campuses and courses, and automates the creation of upload-ready files for populating Desire2Learn (D2L) course templates.

Designed to reduce redundancy and enable precise communication, this tool addresses critical inefficiencies in cross-campus coordination and Learning Management System (LMS) setup.

Key Goals

  • Identify which faculty are teaching at which campuses (Alpharetta, Online, Clarkston, etc.)
  • Track who is teaching each individual course (e.g., MATH 1001, MATH 1111)
  • Generate reverse lookups: given a campus, what courses are being taught and by whom
  • Support targeted communication (e.g., emailing all instructors of a specific course)
  • Create upload-ready .txt files for bulk addition of faculty to shared D2L course templates

Core Features

  • Schedule Ingestion: Converts CSV schedule report into finalized Excel report of faculty teaching assignments with contact information
  • Campus/Course Indexing: Generates multi-level groupings by instructor, campus, and course
  • D2L Uploader Integration: Outputs formatted text files for batch upload to D2L LMS course templates
  • Multi-Campus Compatibility: Handles instructors teaching at multiple sites and in online sections
  • Comms Utility: Enables selective contact lists for distribution of syllabi updates, curricular memos, assessment procedures, etc.

Tools Used

  • Python (pandas, numpy, xlsxwriter)
  • Google Colab (cloud notebook interface)
  • Excel (input/output formatting)

Impact

  • Eliminated repetitive manual entry of faculty into D2L templates across 6 campuses.
  • Accelerated faculty coordination workflows for curriculum committee communications.
  • Ensured accurate and consistent instructor tracking across course offerings.
